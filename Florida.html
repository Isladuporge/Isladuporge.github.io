Between April-August 2025, I performed a controlled experimental study involving ten focal captive lions at Lion Country Safari in West Palm Beach, Florida. Simulated prey (mechanized lures) were towed along a variety of pre-defined trajectories designed to mimic natural prey escape paths, while the lions’ pursuit behaviors were recorded using an overhead UAV. These trials elicited naturalistic hunting responses from across sexes, producing a rich dataset of over sixty high-resolution UAV videos. This dataset is currently being analyzed using pose estimation software, which will offer novel insights into the geometric principles and physical laws that govern lion attack behavior (Video example). This fieldwork part of the project generated significant media interest: CBS-12/ Sun Sentinel/ Florida Weekly/ Palm Beach Post/. A peer-reviewed article documenting the findings is forthcoming.
