We have been using very high-resolution (30 cm) WorldView-3 satellite imagery (Maxar Technologies) to track whales in regions where traditional surveys are challenging, including breeding Southern Right Whales off Southern Africa (image on left) and Beluga whales in the Canadian Arctic (image on right). By training automated detection models (YOLOv12), we can identify individual whales in satellite images, even in remote or ice-covered waters. Because training data are limited, we generate synthetic examples using diffusion models to improve detection accuracy. This approach has the potential to support population assessments, track seasonal movements, and reduce risks from threats such as ship strikes and industrial activity. An academic paper reporting our findings on improving detection performance using diffusion models and YOLOv12 is forthcoming.
